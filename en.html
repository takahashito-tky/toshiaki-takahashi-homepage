Research Overview

Toshiaki TAKAHASHI works across nursing engineering, remote nursing, ICT, nursing education, extended reality, image processing, robotics nursing, and ultrasound imaging. His research aims to visualize the quality of nursing care by translating observation, judgment, procedures, and communication into evaluable knowledge using AI, sensors, image processing, and real-world data for education, clinical practice, and home care.

Profile

Connecting Nursing and Technology

Toshiaki TAKAHASHI works across nursing engineering, remote nursing, ICT, nursing education, extended reality, image processing, robotics nursing, and ultrasound imaging to visualize the quality of care.

Research Statement

Nursing care is an advanced practice that observes human conditions, reads context, and connects decisions to appropriate actions. Because the quality of care and professional skills are often difficult to see, evaluate, and share, this page introduces research that uses AI, sensors, image processing, ICT, and remote support technologies to make nursing care safer, more reproducible, and easier to learn.

Research

Research Themes

The central aim is to develop methods that measure, support, and return the quality of care to practice.

01

Care Quality Visualization

Care processes and outcomes are captured from records, images, sensors, and real-world data and translated into evaluable knowledge.

02

Nursing Skill Assessment and Education Support

Skills in home nursing, peripheral intravenous therapy, wound care, and related fields are objectively evaluated using video, checklists, and AI.

03

Ultrasound, Image Processing, and AI

Research includes ultrasound-based assessment, vascular access support, constipation evaluation, and care recommendation systems.

04

Remote Nursing and Home Nursing DX

The goal is to implement care support using remote monitoring and ICT in medically underserved and home-care settings.
